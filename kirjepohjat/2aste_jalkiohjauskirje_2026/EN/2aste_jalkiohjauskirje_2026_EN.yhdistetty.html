Dear Applicant,

You participated in the spring 2026 joint application to upper secondary education and preparatory education. At this time, you have not been accepted for any of the programmes you applied for. If you are on a waiting list, you may still be admitted to a study place if one becomes available. Information about the status of your application preferences in the student admission process is provided at the end of this letter. Student admissions are carried out in accordance with the regulations issued by the Ministry of Education and Culture concerning the criteria for student admission. Information about the admission criteria and scoring factors can be found in Studyinfo under Joint Application Admission Criteria.

If You Did Not Receive a Study Place

Educational institutions may organise rolling admissions to fill study places that remain vacant after the national joint application. Information about available programmes and application instructions for rolling admission can be found on the institutions’ own websites and in Studyinfo. Long programmes at folk high schools can be found on Studyinfo or on the kansanopistot.fi website.

Obligation to Apply

Students subject to compulsory education must apply, before completing the final year of basic education, to upper secondary education, transitional education, or other education covered by compulsory education. If a student subject to compulsory education does not receive a study place through the joint application, the obligation to apply continues until a study place is secured.

Guidance and Support

If you are completing basic education or preparatory education, you will receive guidance and support from your own guidance counsellor during the summer to help you find a study place. If you are subject to compulsory education and remain without a study place after the summer, you will receive guidance from your municipality of residence.

Ohjaamo is a service for all under 30-year-olds, offering free support and guidance on a wide range of matters, from education and employment to housing and everything in between.

How to Read the Table

Your Application Preferences

All your application preferences in the order you listed them on your application.

Accepted / All Applicants

The number of applicants accepted to the programme in relation to the total number of applicants.

Your Score / Lowest Accepted Score

The total score consists of several components. Information about the admission criteria and scoring factors can be found in Studyinfo under Joint Application Admission Criteria. The lowest accepted score is the minimum score with which applicants have been admitted to the programme. If you applied with a foreign certificate, without a school-leaving certificate, or if you studied mathematics and your mother tongue according to an individualised curriculum, no score will be calculated for you.

If you applied for vocational education provided with demanding special support, preparatory education and training for work and independent living (TELMA), preparatory education for vocational education (TUVA), a folk high school study year programme or general upper secondary education in English, no score will be calculated for you.

Entrance and Aptitude Test Result

If you participated in an entrance or aptitude test, your test score is shown here. If you applied with a foreign certificate, without a school-leaving certificate, or with an individualised curriculum in mathematics and your mother tongue, you applied through discretionary admission, and no score will be calculated for you. Further information about the results can be obtained directly from the institution where you took the test.

Admission Result

The admission result may be accepted, accepted through discretionary admission, on a waiting list, rejected, or cancelled. The result is marked as cancelled if you have been accepted to a higher-ranked application preference.

If your status is on a waiting list, your waiting list position is shown in this column. If your result is rejected or cancelled, you cannot be on a waiting list.

Additional Information on Admission Results

Tie-Breaking in Vocational Education

In the event of a tie, applicants to vocational education are ranked according to the following criteria:

  1. Order of application preferences
  2. Score from any entrance or aptitude test
  3. Overall academic performance
  4. Weighted grades

After this, applicants with identical scores are admitted in a random order.

Tie-Breaking in General Upper Secondary Education

Upper secondary schools admit students based on grade point average. If several applicants have the same average, the admission is decided by:

  1. Order of application preferences
  2. Grade point average, calculated to include academic subjects as well as physical education, crafts, home economics, music, and visual arts

After this, applicants with identical scores are admitted in a random order.

Discretionary Admission

The education provider may admit an applicant regardless of score for a special reason. If you applied through discretionary admission due to social reasons, learning difficulties, or insufficient language skills, you also participate in the normal score-based admission process. If your prior education was completed abroad, you do not have a school-leaving certificate, or you studied mathematics and your mother tongue according to an individualised curriculum, you can only be accepted through discretionary admission. In this case, the admission result is either accepted through discretionary admission or not accepted.

Contact Information

Further information is available in Studyinfo (opintopolku.fi). If you need help interpreting the result letter or if there are uncertainties related to the admission that cannot be resolved by contacting the institution directly, you may contact Studyinfo application advisory services at (neuvonta@opintopolku.fi or by phone at 029 533 1010). Telephone advisory services are available on 11–12th of June from 9:30 a.m. to 1:00 p.m.

Right of Appeal

If you are dissatisfied with the result of the student admission, you may request rectification of the admission decision from the Licensing and Supervisory Authority. To do so, you must request a written decision from the educational institution whose admission decision you wish to appeal. The decision must be requested within 30 days of receiving notification of this result. The request for rectification must be submitted within 14 days of receiving the decision. An appeal for rectification concerning a student admission decision must be submitted to the Licensing and Supervisory Authority. The request for rectification is made using an electronic form, but it can also be sent by email to kirjaamo@lvv.fi or by post to Licensing and Supervisory Authority, P.O. Box 20, FI-13035 LVV, Finland. More detailed instructions can be found on the website of the Licensing and Supervisory Authority. If, for any reason, you do not receive the decision from the institution, please contact Studyinfo application advisory services.
