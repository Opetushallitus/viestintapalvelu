Dear applicant,

You participated in the spring 2026 joint application to upper secondary education and preparatory education. At this time, you have not been accepted for any of the programmes you applied for. If you are on a waiting list, you may still be admitted to a study place if one becomes available. Information about the status of your application preferences in the student admission process is provided at the end of this letter. Student admissions are carried out in accordance with the regulations issued by the Ministry of Education and Culture concerning the criteria for student admission. Information about the admission criteria and scoring factors can be found in Studyinfo under Joint Application Admission Criteria.

If You Did Not Receive a Study Place

Educational institutions may organise rolling admissions to fill study places that remain vacant after the national joint application. Information about available programmes and application instructions for rolling admission can be found on the institutions’ own websites and in Studyinfo. Long programmes at folk high schools can be found on Studyinfo or on the kansanopistot.fi website.

Obligation to Apply

Students subject to compulsory education must apply, before completing the final year of basic education, to upper secondary education, transitional education, or other education covered by compulsory education. If a student subject to compulsory education does not receive a study place through the joint application, the obligation to apply continues until a study place is secured.

Guidance and Support

If you are completing basic education or preparatory education, you will receive guidance and support from your own guidance counsellor during the summer to help you find a study place. If you are subject to compulsory education and remain without a study place after the summer, you will receive guidance from your municipality of residence.

Ohjaamo is a service for all under 30-year-olds, offering free support and guidance on a wide range of matters, from education and employment to housing and everything in between.
