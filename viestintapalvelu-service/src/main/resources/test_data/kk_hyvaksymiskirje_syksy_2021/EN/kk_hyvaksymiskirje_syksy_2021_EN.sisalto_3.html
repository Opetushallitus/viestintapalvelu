Accepting the offer of admission and registration for studies

Remember to accept the offer of admission and register as a student by the date given or you will lose the study place. (Universities Act 38 § and 43 § / Polytechnics Act 28c § 32 §). Accept the offer of admission preferably electronically with the link that has been sent to your email or in the My Studyinfo -service. After you have accepted the offer of admission, you can register as a student in the higher education institution either electronically (the link to the registration will open after you have accepted the offer of admission in the My Studyinfo -service) or in the way indicated by the higher education institution.

Accepting the offer of admission

You must confirm the study place within 7 days after you have been accepted to the study place. Check the deadline on the first page. You can only accept the offer of admission once and you cannot change the answer you have made afterwards. You may accept only one offer of admission in a study programme leading to a higher education degree in Finland in education that begins in the same academic term (1st January to 31st July 2022). When you accept an offer of admission other admission offers will be cancelled automatically. You cannot be offered admission to other programmes starting in the same academic term after you have accepted an offer of admission. The one place per term provision applies to both joint application and separate applications in higher education (transfer student applications are not included in the provision).

You can accept the offer of admission electronically with the link that has been sent to your email. If you have Finnish online banking credentials, a mobile certificate or a smart ID card and a reader, you can accept the offer by logging in to the My Studyinfo -service from the link on top of the Studyinfo.fi front page. The My Studyinfo -service has instructions on how to accept the offer of admission electronically. Link to My Studyinfo -service.

If you cannot accept the offer of admission electronically, contact the higher education institution from which you want to accept an offer of admission. Make sure that the higher education institution receives your written confirmation no later than before 3rd December 2021 at 3 pm (UTC+2). If the confirmation arrives to the higher education institution late, you will lose the study place.

When you accept the offer of admission, you will not be considered a first-time applicant in future applications. More information in Studyinfo.fi: Quota for first-time applicants. After you have accepted the offer of admission, you must also register as a student to the higher education institution by the date given by the higher education institution or you will lose your right to study.

Registering as a student

You can register as a student in the higher education institution either electronically through the national registration service when you accept the offer of admission or in another way indicated by the higher education institution.

If you accept the offer of admission but remain on the waiting list for an offer of admission higher on your preference list, you can only register as an attending or a non-attending student after your offer of admission is accepted and you will not be offered admission by the higher education institution. More detailed instructions on how to register can be found from the registration service and the higher education institution you have applied to. See the link on chapter: "Instructions for new students".

NB! If you are required to pay tuition fees, you cannot register for the academic year before you have completed your payment. You can check this from the higher education institution.

You can register as non-attending for the first academic year only for statutory reasons, that is military or civilian service, maternity leave, paternity leave or parental leave or due to illness or disability. Not completing the registration will lead to forfeiting the right to study (Universities Act § 43 / Polytechnics Act § 32).
